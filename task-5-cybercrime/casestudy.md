# Task 5 – Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Case Study

### Background
Rahul, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, listed his old bicycle for sale on OLX for ₹3,500. Within an hour, he received a message from someone claiming to be an Army officer interested in buying it immediately.

### How It Happened – Step by Step

1. **Initial contact:** The fraudster messaged Rahul expressing urgent interest and said he could not meet in person due to his posting.
2. **Building trust:** He spoke politely and shared a fake Army ID card over WhatsApp to appear legitimate.
3. **The trick:** The fraudster told Rahul, "I will send the payment via Google Pay. Please scan this QR code to receive the money."
4. **The deception:** The QR code was actually a payment request, not a payment link. Scanning and entering his UPI PIN caused ₹3,500 to be debited from Rahul's account.
5. **Escalation:** The fraudster then called again claiming there was a "technical issue" and asked Rahul to scan again — this time for ₹7,000. Rahul lost a total of ₹10,500 before realising he had been scammed.
6. **Disappearance:** The fraudster's number was immediately switched off.

### Who Is Usually Targeted
Young students, first-time online sellers, and elderly individuals with limited knowledge of how UPI payment requests work.

### Consequences
Rahul lost ₹10,500 with no way to reverse the transactions. He filed a complaint on cybercrime.gov.in but did not get a full refund. The emotional impact — embarrassment and loss of trust in digital payments — lasted several months.

*This is a realistic fictional scenario based on a commonly reported fraud pattern in India.*
