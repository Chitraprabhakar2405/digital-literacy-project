# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
## For College Students in India

### General Online Safety

1. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or password with anyone** – No bank, app, or government agency will ever ask for these. Not even Google Pay or PhonePe support staff.
2. **A QR code is for PAYING, not receiving money** – If someone asks you to "scan a code to receive payment," it is a scam. You only scan to pay, never to get paid.
3. **Verify the identity of buyers/sellers before any transaction** – Use video calls, check their profile history, and meet in a public place for cash transactions when possible.
4. **Do not click links sent via WhatsApp or SMS claiming to be from banks or UPI apps** – Always open apps directly from your phone, not from forwarded links.
5. **Enable transaction alerts and set a daily UPI transaction limit** – Most UPI apps allow you to cap daily transfers. Set a low limit to reduce losses in case of fraud.
6. **Check the recipient's name before confirming any UPI transfer** – Always verify the name shown on screen matches the person you intend to pay.


### Financial / UPI-Specific Safety *(Mandatory)*

7. **Never accept "advance payment" offers that ask you to pay first** – Scammers often promise large amounts but ask you to send a small amount first as "verification." This is always fraud.
8. **If you receive money accidentally from an unknown source, do not spend it** – Contact your bank immediately. Fraudsters sometimes send small amounts to build trust before stealing larger sums.


### Reporting

9. **If you are a victim of online fraud, act within the first hour** – Call the **National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** (available 24x7) to freeze the transaction before funds are transferred further.
10. **File a formal complaint at** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) – You can report financial fraud, cyberbullying, and other online crimes here. Keep your transaction ID, screenshots, and the fraudster's number ready.
