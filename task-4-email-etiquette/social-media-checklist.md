# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

##  DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests.
2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?"
3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review who can see your posts, friend list, and personal information on all platforms.
4. **Give credit when sharing content** – If you share someone else's work, article, or image, always tag or acknowledge the original creator.
5. **Engage positively and constructively** – Comment and interact in a respectful, thoughtful manner, especially in academic or professional groups.
6. **Verify information before sharing** – Check whether news or claims are from a reliable source before reposting or forwarding.
7. **Use separate accounts for personal and professional use** – Consider having a LinkedIn for professional networking and keeping personal social media private.

---

##  DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or daily schedule on public profiles.
2. **Don't post during exams or college events** – Live-posting your location or attendance can create security and academic integrity risks.
3. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** – Do not participate in online harassment, and report it if you witness it.
4. **Don't use social media to vent about college, faculty, or peers** – Complaints posted publicly can have serious academic and professional consequences.
5. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** – Unknown accounts may be fake profiles used for phishing or scamming.
6. **Don't share OTPs, passwords, or banking details** – No legitimate person or service will ask for these over social media.
7. **Don't plagiarise or repost content without permission** – Sharing others' work as your own is unethical and can violate copyright laws.
