## Reflection 
For this task, I worked with two platforms: HackerRank for coding practice and Google Forms for collaborative quiz creation.

On HackerRank, I completed the "Solve Me First" challenge, which involves writing a simple function to add two numbers. Although it was a beginner-level problem, it helped me understand how the platform works — how to read input, write a solution, and check test cases. The platform also shows a score and a badge upon completion, which makes it motivating to continue.

For Part B, I built a 5-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms. I included multiple choice and short answer questions to cover a range of response types. The linked Google Sheet automatically collects all responses, making it easy to review what batchmates know about digital safety.

These tools will be very useful academically. HackerRank will help me practise coding problems for placements and competitive programming. Google Forms is already useful for collecting data — I can use it for group surveys, event registrations, and peer feedback during projects. Both platforms are free and browser-based, which makes them accessible to all students.
